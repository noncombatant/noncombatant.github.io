Adventures And Discoveries In Music: Issue 01

14 June 2016

Hello, and welcome to the first issue of Adventures And Discoveries In Music! In this roughly-monthly newsletter I’ll recount wacky tales of what I’ve been up to musically, and point to cool things other people have been up to that I’ve discovered.

Please feel free to forward this to anyone who might be interested, and please let me know if you’re no longer interested, and I’ll remove you from the list.

The archive of all issues is available on the web, of course.

Adventures!

First, my latest adventure: I spent the first week of June up in Vancouver building an electric guitar from scratch, with luthier Meredith Coloma. I posted a bit more about it, including lots of pictures and a sound sample.

Second, my band Rara Replica was in the studio a few weekends back (and some months before that) recording 5 new songs first with our friend Camilo Landau and then again with our friend Jamie Hill. We shall release these nü choonz in due time... (Katya’s and my previous band, Dot Punto., also recorded 2 fine EPs with Camilo and Jamie.)

The third and final adventure in this issue is that I’ve been releasing some electronic music. I half-compose, half-improvise these pieces on a sequencer app for the iPad by pre-planning some sequences, and then record the audio as I tweak the sequences in real time. They are a bit weird, but to me they have a nice boop.

Discoveries!

Composer and performer Ty Braxton had a great interview in The Atlantic: How Tyondai Braxton Subverts and Destroys His Own Music.

Braxton: I don’t think I can ever go back to rock-based music, but I do know that I’ll always utilize guitar in some way, in my orchestral music. The rock-band model for me at this point — I hesitate to make a blanket statement, but it’s certainly not something that really interests me right now.

I got curious about Gregorian chant, and found the Petrucci music library, which has freely-available scores for chant. Then I needed, and found, a cool tutorial on how to read ancient notation. Liquescent neumes!
