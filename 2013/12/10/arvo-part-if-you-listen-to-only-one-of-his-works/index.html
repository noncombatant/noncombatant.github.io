Arvo Pärt

10 December 2013

My favorite of his is Kanon Pokajanen.

Perhaps I’m inclined to favor it since the libretto is written in a borderline-artlang.

There can be some Kierkegaardian wallowing in some of his music. But Kanon Pokajanen is beautiful and powerful from start to finish. There’s a simple purity to choral music; there is no chance to hide compositional flaws in fashion or timbre. Any beauty is true human beauty, any flaw is a real flaw.

Almost all of the music I love is machine musica b—c dbut if I had my life to live over again, I know what I would do: compose music, and mostly for voices.
