The Great Transceiver

The Great Transceiver is a plan for improvising music in a group setting.

To start a song, 1 player (the leader for this song) tells the other players and the audience a word or phrase that inspires them. Perhaps from a book or poem, or the newspaper, or anything. Then the leader starts playing something the other players and the audience have never heard. Nobody has rehearsed anything for the piece, except perhaps the leader.

The song can last anywhere from 30 seconds to 30 minutes, as necessary. A session is complete when every player has been the leader once.
